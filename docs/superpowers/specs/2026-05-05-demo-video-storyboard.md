## Tools

- **Screen recording:** OBS Studio (free, Win/Mac). Record at 1920×1080, 60fps, browser zoom 110%.
- **Editing:** CapCut Desktop (free). Use the built-in beat-detect for auto-snap cuts.
- **Music:** Pixabay Music — free, no attribution required, commercial use OK. Search terms: `sport hype`, `trailer beat`, `motivation`, `epic sport hip hop`. Aim for 120–140 BPM with a build at ~0:45 and drop at ~0:48. Backup source: YouTube Audio Library.

## Pre-record prep

- Use seed data that looks aspirational: stats like 24g/12a, 3.9 GPA, top reach school like UNC or Stanford.
- Hide dev tools, bookmarks bar, browser extensions; use a clean Chrome profile.
- Pre-write the Beeko question and a clean coach email so generations look fast and on-message.
- Use a polished test athlete account — no half-filled fields, no placeholder photos.
- Confirm every animation/transition runs smoothly at 60fps before recording.

## Edit moves that make it look sick

- Every cut snaps to a beat. Land the biggest reveals (rating, email finishing, end card) on the downbeat.
- Quick zoom-ins on the moment of value: the email finishing, the rating revealing, the match dropping.
- Hard cuts only. No dissolves. One whip-pan max.
- Text overlays: huge sans-serif, all lowercase, kinetic in/out (CapCut "Typewriter" or "Slide Up" presets).
- Light filter: slight saturation bump, mild contrast, cool tone.
- End card: logo holds 2s on the drop tail. Silence after the drop = punch.

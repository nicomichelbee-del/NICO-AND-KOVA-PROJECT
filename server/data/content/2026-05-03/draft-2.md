# Draft 2 — Parent perspective: your kid is a solid player, but you don't know which D1, D2, D3, NAIA schools to target — the school matcher cuts through the noise in 60 seconds.

**Format:** both · **Audience:** parent

## Hook (0-1.5s)
> Your daughter's a good player. But which colleges actually want her?

## Suggested Sound
- **Emotional piano (sad-to-hopeful arc)** (`emotional-piano`)
- Vibe: vulnerable, story-driven, tearjerker
- Example to search in app: Ludovico Einaudi-style trends, or 'Until I Found You'-type ballads
- Why: Parent journey (confusion → clarity) is emotional and relatable; slow burn builds to relief when school list appears; piano conveys trust and wisdom.

## Script
- **0-1.5s** — Parent (mom or dad) sitting on couch at home, phone in hand, looking confused, surrounded by college stickers and recruiting brochures
  - VO: "Your daughter's a good player. But which colleges actually want her?"
- **1.5-4s** — Parent scrolling through college websites — takes 5 seconds per school, sighs, closes app
  - VO: "You've looked at 40 D1 websites. Your kid's played in 15 ID camps. But you still don't have a real list."
- **4-7s** — Phone screen recording: parent enters athlete's stats (GPA, club level, position, GPA, test scores) into KickrIo school matcher
  - VO: "Here's what changes: you answer 5 quick questions about your kid's grades, club level, and position. The matcher shows you 50+ schools that actually recruit her profile."
- **7-10s** — Results appear on screen — schools organized by division, match percentage, location. Parent's eyes widen, nods
  - VO: "D1 schools, D2 pipeline options, realistic D3 targets, NAIA safeties. It's not guessing anymore. It's strategy."
- **10-12s** — Parent smiling, showing phone to athlete (in background), both looking relieved

## On-screen Text
- **0-2s** [top] "40 WEBSITES. STILL NO LIST."
- **3-6s** [middle] "5 questions. 50+ matches."
- **7-10s** [middle] "D1 • D2 • D3 • NAIA"
- **10-12s** [bottom] "No more guessing. Just strategy."
